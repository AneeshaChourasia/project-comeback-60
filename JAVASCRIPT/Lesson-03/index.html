JavaScript Lesson 03

Topic: Arithmetic Operators

What is an Operator?

An operator is a special symbol used to perform mathematical or logical operations on values and variables.

Example:

10 + 20 = 30

Here,

  • 10 and 20 are Operands.
  • + is the Operator.

Types of Arithmetic Operators

  1. Addition (+)

    Used to add two numbers.

    Example:

    10 + 20 = 30

  2. Subtraction (-)

    Used to subtract one number from another.

    Example:

    20 - 10 = 10

  3. Multiplication (*)

    Used to multiply two numbers.

    Example:

    5 × 4 = 20

  4. Division (/)

    Used to divide one number by another.

    Example:

    20 ÷ 4 = 5

  5. Modulus (%)

    Returns the remainder after division.

    Example:

    10 % 3 = 1

  6. Exponentiation (**)

    Used to calculate the power of a number.

    Example:

    2 ** 3 = 8

Summary

  • + : Addition
  • - : Subtraction
  • * : Multiplication
  • / : Division
  • % : Modulus (Remainder)
  • ** : Exponentiation (Power)
